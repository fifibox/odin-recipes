Cacio e pepe

Cacio e pepe is a simple Roman pasta dish that is perfect for a quick weeknight dinner. The key to this recipe is to toss the pasta and cheese quickly to create a creamy sauce. The heat from the pasta will melt the cheese and create a smooth sauce. The dish is traditionally made with Pecorino Romano cheese, but Parmigiano-Reggiano can also be used.

Ingredients

4 servings:

  • 320g spaghetti
  • 100g Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Black pepper

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook until al dente.
  2. Meanwhile, grate the Pecorino Romano cheese and set aside.
  3. Drain the pasta, reserving a cup of the cooking water. Add the pasta back to the pot and place over low heat.
  4. Add the cheese to the pasta and toss quickly to combine. If the sauce is too thick, add a little of the reserved cooking water.
  5. Divide the pasta among plates and top with freshly cracked black pepper.
