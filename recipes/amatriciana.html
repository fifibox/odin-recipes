Amatriciana

Amatriciana is a traditional Italian pasta sauce based on guanciale (cured pork cheek), pecorino cheese, and tomato. Originating from the town of Amatrice (in the mountainous Province of Rieti of Lazio region), the Amatriciana is one of the best known pasta sauces in Roman and Italian cuisine. The Italian government has named it a traditional agro-alimentary product of Lazio.

Ingredients

4 servings:

  • 150g guanciale (cured pork jowl), pancetta, or bacon
  • 1/2 teaspoon fresh or crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 400g can of peeled tomatoes
  • 320g bucatini or spaghetti
  • Salt
  • Pecorino cheese

Steps

  2. Meanwhile, chop the guanciale into small pieces and add to a large skillet over medium heat. Cook until the fat has rendered and the guanciale is crispy.
